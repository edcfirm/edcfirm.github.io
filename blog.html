Transformer Oil in Power Transformers: Functions, Types and Properties

Author: Md. Rabiul Hasan Mazumder | March 28, 2026

Transformer oil, also known as insulating oil, is used in oil-filled transformers to provide electrical insulation and cooling. It has excellent dielectric strength, good thermal conductivity and high stability at elevated temperatures.

Functions of Transformer Oil
  • Electrical insulation between transformer windings
  • Cooling medium for heat dissipation
  • Suppression of corona and electrical arcing
  • Protection of cellulose insulation from oxidation
Types of Transformer Oil

The two main categories historically used in power systems are:

  • PCB Oil (Polychlorinated Biphenyl) – Previously used due to non-flammability but banned due to environmental hazards.
  • Mineral Transformer Oil – The most commonly used insulating oil derived from petroleum refining.

Mineral transformer oils are mainly:

  • Paraffinic based oil
  • Naphthenic based oil
Important Properties
  • High dielectric strength
  • Low viscosity
  • High flash point
  • Low moisture content
  • High oxidation stability
Relevant Standards
  • IEC 60296 – Fluids for Electrotechnical Applications
  • ASTM D3487 – Standard Specification for Mineral Insulating Oil
  • IEEE C57.106 – Guide for Acceptance and Maintenance of Insulating Oil

Earthing System in Electrical Power Systems

Author: Md. Rabiul Hasan Mazumder | March 28, 2026

An earthing system provides a safe path for fault current to flow to the ground. Proper earthing design is critical for protecting equipment, ensuring human safety and maintaining system stability in electrical installations and substations.

Purpose of Earthing
  • Protection of personnel from electric shock
  • Safe dissipation of fault current
  • Stabilization of system voltage
  • Protection of electrical equipment
Common Types of Earthing Systems
  • Plate Earthing
  • Rod Earthing
  • Strip or Wire Earthing
  • Grid Earthing used in substations
Earthing Design Standards
  • IEEE 80 – Guide for Safety in AC Substation Grounding
  • IEC 60364 – Electrical Installations of Buildings
  • BNBC Electrical Code

Proper earthing system design requires soil resistivity analysis, grid conductor sizing and step-touch voltage calculations to ensure safety.
