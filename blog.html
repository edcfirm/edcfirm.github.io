Substation Earthing Grid Design According to IEEE 80

Author: Md. Rabiul Hasan Mazumder | March 29, 2026

Earthing systems are a critical component of electrical substations. A properly designed grounding grid ensures that fault currents are safely dissipated into the earth while maintaining safe voltage levels for personnel and equipment.

Why Grounding Design Is Important
  • Protects personnel from dangerous touch voltage.
  • Reduces step voltage on the ground surface.
  • Provides a safe path for fault current.
  • Protects equipment during fault conditions.
Engineering Principles

The objective of grounding system design is to create a low resistance path between electrical equipment and earth while limiting voltage gradients on the ground surface.

Typical Design Procedure
  • Soil resistivity measurement
  • Maximum fault current calculation
  • Grounding grid layout design
  • Step and touch voltage analysis
  • Grounding conductor sizing
Relevant Standards
  • IEEE 80 – Guide for Safety in AC Substation Grounding
  • IEEE 81 – Guide for Measuring Earth Resistivity

Soil Resistivity Measurement Using Wenner Method

Author: Md. Rabiul Hasan Mazumder | March 29, 2026

Soil resistivity measurement is an essential step in the design of grounding systems. The electrical properties of soil directly influence the performance of earthing electrodes and grounding grids.

Importance of Soil Resistivity
  • Determines grounding grid resistance
  • Influences step and touch voltage levels
  • Helps optimize grounding system cost
  • Identifies layered soil structures
Wenner Four-Pin Method

The Wenner method is widely used for soil resistivity testing. Four equally spaced probes are inserted in the soil and current is injected through the outer probes while voltage is measured between the inner probes.

Testing Procedure
  • Place four probes in a straight line
  • Maintain equal spacing between probes
  • Inject current through outer probes
  • Measure voltage between inner probes
Relevant Standards
  • IEEE 81 – Guide for Measuring Earth Resistivity
  • IEC 62561 – Lightning Protection Components

Author: Md. Rabiul Hasan Mazumder | March 30, 2026

Why Short Circuit Studies Are Required
  • Equipment rating verification

Fault current magnitude depends on system impedance, network configuration and generator contribution.

  • Development of impedance model
  • Calculation of symmetrical short circuit current

Transformer Oil in Power Transformers: Functions, Types and Properties

Author: Md. Rabiul Hasan Mazumder | March 28, 2026

Transformer oil, also known as insulating oil, is used in oil-filled power transformers to provide electrical insulation and cooling. The oil has high dielectric strength, excellent thermal conductivity and good chemical stability at elevated temperature.

Functions of Transformer Oil
  • Provides electrical insulation between transformer windings.
  • Acts as a cooling medium by transferring heat from windings to transformer tank and radiators.
  • Suppresses corona discharge and electrical arcing.
  • Protects cellulose insulation from oxidation.
Types of Transformer Oil

Historically two major types of transformer oil were used in electrical equipment:

  • Polychlorinated Biphenyl (PCB) Oil – Previously used because of its non-flammable nature, but later banned due to environmental hazards.
  • Mineral Transformer Oil – The most widely used insulating oil in modern transformers.

Mineral transformer oil can be categorized as:

  • Paraffinic based oil
  • Naphthenic based oil
Important Properties
  • High dielectric strength
  • Low viscosity
  • High flash point
  • Low moisture content
  • High oxidation stability
Relevant Standards
  • IEC 60296 – Fluids for Electrotechnical Applications
  • ASTM D3487 – Standard Specification for Mineral Insulating Oil
  • IEEE C57.106 – Guide for Acceptance and Maintenance of Insulating Oil
