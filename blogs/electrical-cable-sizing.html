Electrical Cable Sizing According to IEC Standards – Practical Design Approach


Electrical cable sizing is one of the most critical aspects of power system design. Incorrect cable selection can lead to overheating, voltage drop, energy loss, and even fire hazards. Proper cable sizing ensures safe and reliable operation of electrical systems.

What is Cable Sizing?

Cable sizing is the process of selecting the appropriate conductor size based on electrical, thermal, and mechanical requirements. It ensures that the cable can safely carry load current under specified operating conditions.

IEC Standards for Cable Sizing

Cable sizing is typically performed according to:

• IEC 60287 – Current carrying capacity calculation
• IEC 60364 – Electrical installation guidelines

Key Factors in Cable Selection

Proper cable sizing depends on several factors:

✔ Load current
✔ Installation method (tray, conduit, underground)
✔ Ambient temperature
✔ Grouping of cables
✔ Soil thermal resistivity (for underground cables)

Main Design Criteria

A cable must satisfy the following conditions:

• Current carrying capacity (ampacity)
• Voltage drop limit
• Short circuit withstand capability

Voltage Drop Consideration

Excessive voltage drop can affect equipment performance. IEC standards recommend limiting voltage drop within acceptable ranges depending on system type and application.

Short Circuit Withstand

Cables must be capable of withstanding thermal stress during fault conditions. Short circuit current and duration are key parameters for this calculation.

Practical Engineering Insight

In real projects, cable sizing is an iterative process. Engineers must consider multiple correction factors and ensure coordination with protection devices. Oversizing increases cost, while undersizing creates safety risks.
